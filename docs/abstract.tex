\cabstract{
    随着云原生技术和微服务架构的普及，现代 IT 系统的复杂性与动态性显著增加，导致传统运维（Ops）面临巨大挑战。站点可靠性工程（SRE）通过将软件工程原则应用于运维实践，旨在提升系统可靠性，但其高度依赖人工分析和经验，难以适应快速变化的云环境。近年来，人工智能运维（AIOps）应运而生，试图利用机器学习和大数据技术自动化运维任务。然而，多数 AIOps 方案侧重于孤立的问题解决，难以实现端到端的自主决策和持续学习。

    为解决上述挑战，本文提出并实现了一个名为 OpsEvolver 的多智能体自主演化 SRE 框架。该框架融合了大语言模型（LLM）的推理能力与混沌工程实践，并以主动学习、探索/利用分离和双系统认知为方法论基础。通过离线演化与在线诊断的紧密结合，OpsEvolver 能够主动探索未知故障空间，并通过自适应反馈环实现知识的持续学习和迭代。在框架中，探索代理负责施加受控扰动以模拟真实故障，诊断代理负责围绕告警组织根因分析，反思代理则负责从诊断经验中提取结构化知识。

    在 Astronomy Shop、Hotel Reservation 和 Social Network 三个微服务系统上，基于 DeepSeek-V3.2 的主实验累计执行了 959 次故障注入，其中 703 次触发了可观测告警并进入诊断流程，进一步沉淀出 718 条知识条目和 768 条规则。实验结果表明，OpsEvolver 能够在真实微服务环境中组织完整的“演练-诊断-反思-沉淀”闭环，并显式学习可观测性边界：Astronomy Shop 更容易暴露时延传播型异常，Hotel Reservation 与 Social Network 则更突出日志主导和级联故障主导的排障模式。进一步的基底模型对比实验在每个系统统一截取前 300 轮注入记录后表明，Llama 4 Maverick 17B Instruct 更有利于扩大可观测覆盖，Qwen3-32B 倾向于生成更高密度的知识与规则，而 DeepSeek-V3.2 在日志主导和级联故障更强的系统上表现出更平衡的严格命中能力。尽管严格根因命中率仍有提升空间，该原型已经证明，借助外部知识记忆、多模态告警建模和反馈驱动演化，可以持续积累环境特定的运维知识，为构建具备自主演化能力的下一代智能运维系统提供可行路径。
}

\eabstract{
    With the proliferation of cloud-native technologies and microservice architectures, the complexity and dynamism of modern IT systems have significantly increased, posing immense challenges to traditional operations (Ops). Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) applies software engineering principles to operations to enhance system reliability, yet its heavy reliance on manual analysis and human experience struggles to adapt to rapidly evolving cloud environments. In recent years, Artificial Intelligence for IT Operations (AIOps) has emerged, seeking to automate operational tasks using machine learning and big data technologies. However, most AIOps solutions focus on isolated problem-solving, making it difficult to achieve end-to-end autonomous decision-making and continuous learning.

    To address these challenges, this thesis proposes and implements OpsEvolver, a multi-agent self-evolving SRE framework. The framework is grounded in active learning, explore-exploit separation, and a dual-process cognition view that combines accumulated experience with runtime reasoning. Through a tight coupling of offline evolution and online diagnosis, OpsEvolver can actively explore unknown fault spaces and achieve continuous knowledge learning and iteration via an adaptive feedback loop. Within the framework, exploratory agents apply controlled perturbations to simulate failures, diagnostic agents conduct root cause analysis through a "Plan-Act-Observe-Reflect" cycle, and reflective agents extract structured knowledge from previous diagnostic experience. A feedback analysis component further maps diagnostic failures back to future learning tasks, enabling self-correction and continuous knowledge refinement.

    Across Astronomy Shop, Hotel Reservation, and Social Network, the main DeepSeek-V3.2-based offline evolution experiments accumulated 959 fault injections, of which 703 triggered observable alerts and entered the diagnosis loop, yielding 718 knowledge items and 768 rules. The results show that OpsEvolver can organize a complete loop of rehearsal, diagnosis, reflection, and knowledge accumulation in real microservice environments, while also learning observability boundaries through no-alert injections. Astronomy Shop exposes latency-propagation patterns more clearly, whereas Hotel Reservation and Social Network reveal stronger log-dominant and cascading-failure behaviors. Additional model-comparison experiments, evaluated under a unified cutoff of the first 300 injections for each system, show that Llama 4 Maverick 17B Instruct improves observability coverage, Qwen3-32B produces denser knowledge and rule extraction, and DeepSeek-V3.2 remains more balanced on log-dominant and cascading systems. Although strict root-cause hit rate still leaves room for improvement, the prototype demonstrates that combining externalized memory, multi-modal alert modeling, and feedback-driven evolution is a feasible path toward autonomous and self-improving SRE systems.
}
